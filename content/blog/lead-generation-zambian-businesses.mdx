We've audited over 40 Zambian websites in the last year. The same five mistakes keep showing up — and each one is costing the business real money.

## Mistake 1: No clear offer on the homepage

Visitors land on your site and within 5 seconds they should know:

1. What you do
2. Who you do it for
3. What to do next

Most Zambian business sites fail at all three. The homepage hero reads like a brochure — "Welcome to ACME Ltd, est. 2010" — instead of "We help private clinics in Lusaka turn walk-ins into bookings."

**Fix:** Rewrite your hero as a single clear sentence that names your audience and the outcome.

## Mistake 2: No lead magnet

Only 2% of people who visit your site are ready to buy today. If you have no way to capture the other 98%, they leave and never return.

A lead magnet is anything that makes it worth giving you an email address — a guide, a checklist, a pricing sheet, a quiz.

**Fix:** Pick one piece of content your best prospects would pay for, and give it away in exchange for an email.

## Mistake 3: Broken booking flow

We see this constantly: a strong ad, a decent landing page, and then — a contact form buried four clicks deep that takes 11 fields to fill out.

Every extra field costs you conversions. Every extra click costs you conversions.

**Fix:** Reduce to the minimum viable form (name, email, phone) and put a booking button on every page.

## Mistake 4: No follow-up automation

Someone fills out your form at 9pm on a Sunday. You see it Monday at 10am. By then, they've already contacted two of your competitors.

**Fix:** Automate the first touch. An instant confirmation email, a calendar link, a text message. The first business to respond wins most deals.

## Mistake 5: No measurement

If you can't answer "how many leads did we get last month from which channel," you're flying blind. You'll keep spending on what doesn't work and underspending on what does.

**Fix:** Set up basic tracking. Even a spreadsheet with source + count + converted is better than nothing.

## The common thread

All five mistakes have the same root cause: there's no **system**. There's a website, maybe some ads, a contact form — but nothing connects them. Each piece is a silo.

A growth system fixes this by treating lead generation as infrastructure, not activity.
